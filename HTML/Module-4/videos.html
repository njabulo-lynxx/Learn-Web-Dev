Inserting Videos in HTML

You can embed videos in your HTML documents using the <video> element. This element allows you to specify multiple video sources, control playback, and provide fallback content for browsers that do not support the video tag.

Basic Video Example

<video width="640" height="360" controls>
    <source src="movie.mp4" type="video/mp4">
    <source src="movie.ogg" type="video/ogg">
    Your browser does not support the video tag.
</video>

Attributes of the <video> Element

  • width: Specifies the width of the video player in pixels.
  • height: Specifies the height of the video player in pixels.
  • controls: Adds video controls like play, pause, and volume.
  • autoplay: The video will start playing as soon as it is ready.
  • loop: The video will restart automatically after reaching the end.
  • muted: The video will be muted by default.

Example with Autoplay and Loop

<video width="640" height="360" autoplay loop muted>
    <source src="movie.mp4" type="video/mp4">
    Your browser does not support the video tag.
</video>
